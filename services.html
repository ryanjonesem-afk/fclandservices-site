What We Do

Land Services Detail

Every job is scoped clearly, priced honestly, and completed correctly. That includes working safely by maintaining awareness of on-site conditions, respecting adjoining properties and existing vegetation, and following applicable environmental and OSHA requirements. The goal isn't just to move dirt — it's to leave a property in better shape than we found it, with drainage that works and ground that holds.

Gravel Driveway Repair & Restoration

Rutted, washed-out, or poorly graded driveways restored for residential and rural clients in Spring Hill KS, Olathe, and across Johnson and Miami County. Work is graded to drain — restoring the crown, correcting adjacent ditches, and placing new rock at the right depth so the surface holds up through the next storm and the one after that.

  • Gravel spreading and leveling to finished grade
  • Driveway crown restoration and regrading
  • Rut, pothole, and washout repair
  • Ditch and drainage correction at the driveway edge
  • New base rock and surface gravel placement
  • Culvert checks and clearing

Precision Grading & Erosion-Conscious Site Work

Grading and leveling for drainage improvement, construction prep, and landscape work in Olathe KS, Spring Hill, and the Kansas City metro. Sites are read carefully before any ground is moved — graded to direct water away from structures and toward stable discharge points, then stabilized so disturbed ground doesn't turn into the next erosion problem.

  • Finish and rough grading
  • Drainage slope correction and yard regrading
  • Construction site prep with erosion control in mind
  • Low-spot and standing-water correction
  • Stabilization with seed, straw, and erosion blanket as needed

Harley Rake & Seedbed Prep / Stabilization

Power raking and soil finish work to prepare a clean, level seedbed before hydroseeding, sod, or turf establishment. Proper prep is the single biggest factor in seeding success — and on disturbed sites, fast stabilization is what stops the next storm from undoing the grading. Skipping either almost always leads to rework.

  • Harley rake / power rake finish work
  • Rock and debris removal
  • Soil pulverizing and leveling
  • Pre-hydroseed and pre-sod site prep
  • Seed, straw, and erosion blanket stabilization

Frequently Asked

Land Services Questions

How much does driveway restoration cost in the Kansas City area?

Cost depends on length, current condition, drainage issues, and whether new gravel or base material is needed. Most residential and rural driveways in the KC metro fall in a predictable range, and we provide a specific written estimate after seeing the site — never a vague range.

Request a free quote or call 913-732-0069.

When is the best time of year for grading and dirt work in Kansas?

Spring through fall is ideal — the ground is workable and stabilization (seed and straw) takes well. We work year-round when conditions allow, but extreme wet, frozen, or saturated ground can delay scheduling for quality reasons.

Do you handle agricultural and rural property work?

Yes. Hay bale moving, acreage mowing, fence-line cleanup, rock removal, small tree and stump removal, debris cleanup, and general rural property work are routine across Miami County, Johnson County, and surrounding areas.

If you're a farmer, rancher, or rural landowner with a property that needs maintained, describe what you're working with and we'll give you an honest answer about scope and cost.

Do you do small tree removal and stump grinding?

Yes. Small tree removal, stump grinding to grade, and debris cleanup are routine. For large or mature trees, we'll point you toward a tree service — but small trees, brush, and stumps are part of normal property cleanup and acreage work.

Can you clear overgrown fence lines and brush on acreage?

Yes. Selective brush clearing, fence-line cleanup, and light land clearing on rural acreage are routine. We work with awareness of property boundaries, neighboring conditions, and any environmental constraints — preserving desirable trees and ground cover and piling or removing debris as the site requires.
