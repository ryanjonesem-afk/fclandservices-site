What is SWPPP and do I need one?

A Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) is required under the NPDES Construction General Permit for most construction sites disturbing one acre or more (and smaller sites that are part of a larger common plan of development).

The SWPPP documents how stormwater runoff and erosion will be controlled, requires routine inspections, and must be maintained on-site. We manage SWPPPs end-to-end — from plan review through final stabilization.

What's the difference between SWPPP and BMP?

A SWPPP is the written plan that documents stormwater compliance for a site. BMPs (Best Management Practices) are the physical controls — silt fence, inlet protection, rock check dams, erosion blankets, sediment basins — that the SWPPP requires to be installed and maintained.

We do both: plan management and field installation.
