Stormwater Control Pond Restoration, Flow Improvement & Stabilization

An overgrown, sediment-filled stormwater channel had significantly reduced flow capacity and storage. Vegetation encroachment and accumulated sediment were choking the system, creating flood risk and regulatory exposure.

Cleared overgrowth, removed accumulated sediment, reshaped and stabilized the channel banks. All work was sequenced and documented to support regulatory compliance and demonstrate that corrective action was completed properly. Seed and straw stabilization applied post-grading to prevent erosion recurrence.

Restored channel to full flow capacity and storage volume. Banks stabilized against future erosion. Work was performed safely and in accordance with applicable site and environmental requirements. Client received a documented, defensible record of corrective action — reducing enforcement risk.

Land Services
Kansas City Metro • Drainage / Erosion Control

Culvert Extension & Erosion Control

An undersized culvert outlet was causing significant erosion at the discharge point. Insufficient outlet length allowed high-velocity flow to scour the surrounding soil, creating an expanding erosion channel.

Extended the culvert outlet to the correct length, installed a properly sized riprap apron to dissipate flow energy, and stabilized disturbed areas with seed and straw. Work completed in a single mobilization.

Erosion stopped at the discharge point. Riprap apron controlling energy and preventing future scouring. Surrounding ground stabilized and vegetating. No further corrective action needed.

Kansas City Metro • Remediation / Environmental Response

Petroleum-Impacted Soil Removal & Oil-Water Separator Repair

Diesel-impacted soil discovered adjacent to a failing oil-water separator. Active leakage had allowed petroleum-impacted material to migrate, increasing the contamination footprint and long-term remediation cost.

Assessed extent of impacted soil, coordinated targeted excavation to remove affected material before further migration, managed waste characterization and disposal through licensed channels, and coordinated OWS repair. Full field documentation maintained throughout to support regulatory closeout.

Migration stopped. Impacted soil removed, properly characterized, and disposed through licensed channels in compliance with applicable waste handling requirements. OWS repaired and operational. Prevented further spread that would have significantly increased long-term remediation costs and regulatory exposure.
