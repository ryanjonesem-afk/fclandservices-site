Frequently Asked

SWPPP & Environmental FAQs

What is a SWPPP and when is it required?

A Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) is required under the EPA NPDES Construction General Permit for most construction sites disturbing one acre or more, and for smaller sites that are part of a larger common plan of development.

The plan documents how runoff and erosion will be controlled. It's paired with routine inspections, must be maintained on-site, and is updated as conditions change.

What does SWPPP management actually involve?

Reviewing and updating the written plan, performing routine compliance inspections (typically weekly and after qualifying rain events), documenting BMP condition with photos, tracking corrective actions, supporting NPDES permit requirements, and communicating with regulators when needed.

We handle all of the above as a single point of contact through final stabilization.

What BMPs do you install?

Silt fence, inlet protection, rock check dams, erosion control blankets, sediment basins and traps, riprap aprons, and culvert extensions. We install them per plan requirements, position and construct them to function correctly, and maintain or repair them on schedule.

We received a Notice of Violation. Can you help?

Yes. NOV response is direct experience. We assess the violation, identify what corrective action is actually required (versus what isn't), execute or coordinate the field work, document everything, and support communication with the regulator through resolution.
