# SECTION 10 — OPERATIONS NOTES

### 10.1 Rent vs. Buy — Attachment Decision Framework

| Attachment | Rent Until… | Buy When… | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Standard bucket | Owned from day 1 | — | Required core |
| Grapple | Day 1 if budget allows; otherwise rent first | Booking ≥6 grapple jobs/month | High-utility — buy early |
| Pallet forks | Owned by month 6 | Material handling becomes routine | Cheap to own |
| Brush cutter | Rent first season | Booking ≥3 brush jobs/month | $7–10K to own; rents at $250/day |
| Stump grinder | Rent first season | Booking ≥2 stump jobs/week | Tooth wear is brutal — verify margin holds |
| Auger | Rent per project until needed monthly | 4+ post-hole jobs per month | Bits are $400–800 each |
| Harley rake | Rent until finish-grade work is ≥10% of revenue | Buy when seeding/lawn-prep work hits steady volume | Premium attachment |
| Land leveler | Rent until needed regularly | Steady gravel-drive work | Often substituted by bucket back-drag |
| Forestry mulcher | **Rent only — for years** | Only when you're booked 100+ hrs/yr at $295/hr | $35–55K attachment, $20K+ wear/yr |

> **Rule:** If you rent an attachment 4+ times in a season, run the math on owning. Below that, the rental margin is yours to keep without the capital tied up.

### 10.2 Best Starter Services (First 90 Days)

Focus capacity on jobs that are **fast, profitable, and visible**:

1. Driveway refresh (your "hero" service)
2. Pothole repair (high-margin, easy yes)
3. Brush pile removal (every rural lead has one)
4. Stump grinding (premium per-hour rate)
5. Property cleanup (volume + repeat)

Avoid early: complex drainage, large demo, tree removal near structures, anything requiring permits.

### 10.3 Best ROI Services (Long Term)

| Service | Why it wins |
|---------|-------------|
| **Driveway refresh & maintenance** | High repeat, photo-friendly, premium pricing, low risk |
| **Acreage mowing (recurring)** | Predictable seasonal revenue, low complexity |
| **Storm cleanup** | Surge pricing, fast cash, reactive sales |
| **Stump grinding** | High $/hr, low capital after grinder is paid off |
| **Bundled property packages** | Higher ticket, easier upsell, fewer mobilizations |

### 10.4 Best Attachment Investments (Order of Priority)

1. **Grapple bucket** — used on nearly every cleanup and brush job
2. **Pallet forks** — cheap, useful for material handling
3. **Brush cutter** — second season after volume confirms
4. **Stump grinder** — buy when stump volume justifies (high wear)
5. **Harley rake** — when finish-grade work becomes regular
6. **Forestry mulcher** — last; rent for years before owning

### 10.5 How to Avoid Underbidding

| Trap | How to avoid |
|------|--------------|
| "I'll just throw a number out" | Always walk the site or request photos/measurements |
| Forgetting mobilization | Build into minimum charge or itemize |
| Ignoring dump fees | Always confirm disposal route before quoting |
| Underestimating brush density | Pad density-based jobs by 15% |
| Forgetting wear/fuel premium per attachment | Use Section 8 rates — never standard $165/hr |
| Quoting too fast on phone | Provide a *range*; firm up after walk-through |
| Matching a lowball competitor | Walk away; you can't out-cheap a guy with no insurance |
| Not building in reschedule risk | Quote per-job, not per-day-on-calendar |
| Forgetting to account for traffic / load times | Add 30 min buffer per material trip |
| Volume discounts that destroy margin | Cap discounts at 10–15%; never bundle below 40% margin |
